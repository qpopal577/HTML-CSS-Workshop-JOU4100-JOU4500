Shutter Speed

Shutter speed controls how fast the shutter closes. A fast shutter speed captures action very well, but lets in less light. Slowing down a shutter speed means you can really do some cool light trick thingies with your camera.

Aperture

Aperture Adjusting the aperture on your camera allows you to blur or focus on subjects. A lower aperture means a blurrier background. This is called depth of field. A blurry background is considered shallow depth of field, I think.
